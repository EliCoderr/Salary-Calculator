Understanding pay, salary, raises, and overtime

A full-time work year is often estimated at 2,080 hours, which comes from 40 hours per week for 52 weeks. That is why salary and hourly conversions often use 2,080 as the baseline. For example, if you earn $20 per hour and work 40 hours each week for 52 weeks, your estimated annual pay is $41,600 before taxes.

A pay raise calculator compares your current hourly rate with your new hourly rate, then multiplies the difference by your hours per week and weeks worked per year. Monthly increase is based on the annual increase divided by 12, so it shows the average monthly gain over a full year.

Overtime is commonly calculated at 1.5 times the regular hourly rate, while double time is 2 times the regular rate. These formulas give a quick weekly estimate based on extra hours only, not total weekly hours.

Hourly to salary example

$25 per hour × 40 hours per week = $1,000 per week. Over 52 weeks, that equals $52,000 per year. Monthly, that comes out to about $4,333.33 before taxes.

Salary to hourly example

A salary of $60,000 divided by 2,080 hours equals about $28.85 per hour. Weekly, that is about $1,153.85 before taxes.

Pay raise example

If your hourly rate increases from $20 to $22 and you work 40 hours per week for 52 weeks, your pay increases by $80 per week, about $346.67 per month, and $4,160 per year.

Overtime example

If you earn $20 per hour, work 40 regular hours, 5 overtime hours, and 2 double-time hours, your weekly pay is $1,030. That includes $800 regular pay, $150 overtime pay, and $80 double-time pay.

Frequently asked questions

How do I convert hourly pay to yearly salary?

Multiply your hourly rate by the number of hours you work per week, then multiply that by the number of weeks you work per year. A common example is 40 hours per week and 52 weeks per year.

How do I convert salary to hourly pay?

Divide your annual salary by the total number of hours worked in a year. For many full-time workers, that means dividing by 2,080 hours, which is 40 hours per week for 52 weeks.

How is a pay raise calculated?

A pay raise calculator compares your current hourly rate with your new hourly rate. It then multiplies the difference by your hours per week and weeks per year to estimate your weekly, monthly, and annual increase.

How is overtime pay usually calculated?

In many jobs, overtime is paid at 1.5 times the regular hourly rate, while double time is paid at 2 times the regular rate. Rules can vary by employer and location.
